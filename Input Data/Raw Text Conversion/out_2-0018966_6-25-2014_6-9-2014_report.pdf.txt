1.0 INTRODUCTION 

 

On behalf of Getty Properties Corp. (Getty), Tyree Environmental Corp. (Tyree) has prepared 

the following Immediate Response Action (IRA) Status Report, for the former Getty Station No. 

30617 located at 528 North Main Street, Leominster, Massachusetts (the Site), in accordance 

with the requirements of section 310 CMR 40.0425 of the Massachusetts Contingency Plan 

(MCP).  Please refer to Figures 1 through 3 for a Site Location Map, Site Plan, and Aerial Map, 

respectively. 

 

For the purposes of this document, the term “Site” or “Disposal Site” shall refer to all locations, 

including the Subject Property, where oil and/or hazardous materials (OHM) have come to be 

located as a result of a release.  This IRA Status Report and Bureau of Waste Site Cleanup 

(BWSC) Form 105 are being submitted electronically to the Massachusetts Department of 

Environmental Protection’s (MassDEP) via the eDEP online filing system.  The time period 

covered by this report is January 1, 2014 through May 31, 2014.  The report is being submitted at 

this time to remain current with the regulatory deadline.  Additional data obtained after May 31, 

2014 will be included in the IRA Status Report due to MassDEP in December 2014. 

 

IRA activities at the Site are being conducted by Getty under the supervision of the Licensed Site 

Professional (LSP) of Record for the Site, Mr. Robert Berger, LSP No. 7565.  In the opinion of 

Tyree, Response Action Performance Standards, as required by the MCP, have been employed 

throughout the duration of this project.   

 

The following reports and/or forms were previously provided to the MassDEP for this release 

tracking number (RTN):  

 

 Release Notification Form August 14, 2013 – RTN 2-18966;

2.0 REGULATORY STATUS 

 

On September 20, 2005, during tank tightness testing, Crompco Corporation (Crompco) located 

in Plymouth Meeting, PA, reported that a 10,000-gallon diesel underground storage tank (UST) 

failed tank tightness testing.  After the failed tank test, on September 21, 2005 at approximately 

9:00 A.M., the MassDEP was immediately notified by a representative of Crompco of the 72-

hour reportable condition.  At this time, the MassDEP subsequently issued Release Tracking 

Number (RTN) 2-15903 for the apparent release condition.  The Site is currently in Phase V 

Remedy Operation Status (ROS).

4.0 SITE CONDITIONS AND SURROUNDING RECEPTORS 

 

The Site is located on the west side of North Main Street (Route 12), near the intersection of 

North Main Street and Route 2.  The Universal Traverse Mercator (UTM) coordinates for the 

Site are 726552 meters west and 4712135 meters north, and the latitudinal and longitudinal 

coordinates for the site are 42.5284° north and 71.7584° west, as derived from the United States 

Geologic Survey (USGS) 7.5 x 15 minute Snipatuit Pond Quadrangle Map (Figure 1).  The Site 

consists of a relatively level parcel of land with an area of approximately 7,563 square feet, 

according to the Assessor’s Office website.  According to the Zoning Map viewed on the City of 

Leominster Assessors Website, the Site is located in a Commercial Zone.  The North Nashua 

River and associated Federal Emergency Management Agency (FEMA) 100-year flood plain 

extends from the northeast to the east of the site and is located at its closest extent approximately 

700 feet east of the Site.   

 

The property is currently an inactive gasoline service station, branded as BP and was formerly 

operated as a Getty gasoline service station.  The current on-Site buildings were constructed in 

1971.  The on-Site buildings at the property consist of a service station kiosk located on the 

south-central portion and a storage building located on the northwest portion.  Information 

obtained during reconnaissance activities at the Site indicated that approximately 95% of the 

Subject Property is either paved or covered by the two on-Site structures.  Two (2) 10,000-gallon 

cathodically-protected single-wall steel gasoline USTs and one (1) 10,000-gallon cathodically-

protected single-wall steel diesel UST were formerly located on the western portion of the Site, 

were removed between August 6 and August 8, 2013.  A Site plan depicting the general layout of 

the property is included as Figure 2.   

 

According to the City of Leominster Water and Sewer Department, the Site is connected to the 

City of Leominster water and sewer systems.   

 

Based on historical records, the Site has been utilized for retail gasoline sales since circa 1967. 

According to a review of available records and Tyree field reconnaissance, no waste oil and fuel 

oil USTs and/or aboveground storage tanks (ASTs) are known to exist at the Site. 

 

A MassDEP BWSC Site Scoring Map was generated online at the Massachusetts Geographic 

Information Systems (MassGIS) website and was included in the October 2013 IRA Plan.  

Identification of natural resources within 500 feet of the property was assessed by reviewing a 

MassGIS Map and through a reconnaissance of the Site and the surrounding area conducted by 

Tyree personnel.  According to a telephone conversation with Mr. Christopher Knuth of the City 

of Leominster Board of Health, properties near the Site are connected to the municipal water and 

sewer system.  No private drinking water wells are known to exist within 500 feet of the Site.   

 

The North Nashua River and associated FEMA 100-year flood plain extends from the northeast 

to the east of the Site and is located at its closest extent approximately 700 feet east of the Site.  

An area defined as Protected Open Space is located approximately 600 feet southwest of the Site.  

An area defined as Non-Potential Drinking Water Source Area is located at its closest extent 0.1-

mile to the east and extends to the northeast and east of the Site.

5.1 Groundwater Gauging and Sampling 

 

A groundwater monitoring well gauging and sampling event was conducted at the Site on March 

25, 2014.  Results of the groundwater gauging activities have been tabulated and summarized in 

Table 1.  Ten (10) monitoring wells (MW-1R, MW-2, MW-3, MW-4, MW-6 through MW-9, 

MW-11, and MW-12) were gauged with an interface probe to determine depth to groundwater 

and depth to light non-aqueous phase liquid (LNAPL), if present.  LNAPL was not detected in 

any of the wells gauged during the March 25, 2014 gauging event.  Depth to water at the 

property ranged from approximately 6.97 fbg (MW-12) to 14.83 fbg (MW-7) during the gauging 

event.  Monitoring well MW-1R was gauged to be dry at a depth of 15.05 fbg.  Monitoring wells 

MW-5 and MW-10 could not be accessed under the snow and ice.  

 

Based on the March 25, 2014 groundwater sampling event, groundwater appears to flow in a 

southerly direction across the Site as depicted on Figure 2A.  Based on the Site topography, it is 

possible that groundwater flow directions may vary to south, west, and northwest during 

seasonal fluctuations in the groundwater table.   

 

On March 25, 2014, monitoring wells (MW-2, MW-3, MW-4, MW-6 through MW-9, MW-11 

and MW-12) were purged of three to five well volumes of groundwater and allowed to recharge 

prior to collecting samples.  All groundwater samples were submitted to Accutest of New 

England (Accutest), located in Marlborough, Massachusetts, a Massachusetts state certified 

laboratory (M-MA136), under standard chain of custody (COC) protocol for volatile petroleum 

hydrocarbons (VPH) analysis via MassDEP methodologies.  Additionally, samples from MW-3, 

MW-8 and MW-9 were analyzed for Monitored Natural Attenuation (MNA) field parameters 

[dissolved oxygen (DO), oxygen reduction potential (ORP), pH, temperature, and conductivity] 

and laboratory parameters (dissolved lead, dissolved manganese, alkalinity, sulfate, sulfide, 

nitrate, nitrite, and methane). The MNA field and laboratory parameters are summarized in Table 

2.

7.1 Imminent Hazards 

 

The MCP (310 CMR 40.0321) identifies releases and threats of releases that pose or could pose 

an Imminent Hazard (IH).  As indicated in 310 CMR 40.0321(1), the following releases shall be 

deemed to pose an IH: 

 

 A release to the environment which results in the presence of OHM within buildings, 
structures, or underground utility conduits at a concentration equal to or greater than 10% 

of the lower explosive limit; 

 A release to the environment of reactive or explosive hazardous material, as described in 
310 CMR 40.0347, which threatens human health or safety; 

 A release to a roadway that endangers public safety; 

 A release to the environment of OHM which poses a significant risk to human health 
when present for even a short period of time, as specified in 310 CMR 40.0950; 

 A release to the environment of OHM which produces immediate or acute adverse 
impacts to freshwater or saltwater populations; or, 

 A release to the environment which produces readily apparent effects to human health, 
including respiratory distress or dermal irritation.

7.2 Critical Exposure Pathways 

 

As defined by 310 CMR 40.0006, CEPs are defined as the routes by which oil or hazardous 

material(s) released at a Site are transported to human receptors via: 

 Vapor-phase emissions of measurable concentrations of OHM into the living or working 
space of a pre-school, daycare, school, or occupied residential dwelling; or, 

 Ingestion, dermal absorption, or inhalation of measurable concentrations of OHM from 
drinking water supply wells located at and servicing a pre-school, daycare, school, or 

occupied residential dwelling.  

Currently, the release conditions associated with RTN 2-18966 do not meet any of the 

aforementioned requirements, and no CEPs have been identified for this RTN.  The Site will 

continue to be monitored for potential CEP conditions. 

 

7.3 Substantial Release Migration 

 

Tyree has evaluated the MCP defined conditions which are indicative of a condition of 

Substantial Release Migration (SRM).  It continues to be the opinion of Tyree that a condition of 

SRM does not currently exist at the Site.  The Site will continue to be assessed and monitored for 

potential SRM conditions. 

 

Tyree has evaluated the MCP defined conditions which are indicative of a Condition of 

Substantial Release Migration.  The defined conditions found in 310 CMR 40.0006 are presented 

below along with Site-specific responses that address the conditions. 

 

a) Releases that have resulted in the discharge of separate-phase oil and/or hazardous 
material to surface waters, subsurface structures, or underground utilities or conduits – 

The recent assessment activities indicate that the releases have not resulted in any known 

discharge of separate-phase oil to surface waters, subsurface structures or underground 

utilities or conduits. 

 

b) Releases to the ground surface or to the vadose zone that, if not promptly removed or 
contained, are likely to significantly impact the underlying groundwater, or significantly 

exacerbate an existing condition of groundwater pollution – Contamination has not been 

observed to migrate any distance greater than that observed soon after the release was 

identified and the contaminant plume is naturally attenuating over time.  Due to the fate 

and transport mechanisms and the observed contaminant distribution and the source 

removal performed, the current condition of release is not expected to significantly 

further impact the underlying groundwater or significantly exacerbate existing 

groundwater pollution.

7.4 Preferential Migration Pathways 

 

Preferential Migration Pathways (PMPs) are preferred pathways by which OHM is transported at 

or from a Disposal Site.   

 

On August 7, 2013, Tyree personnel field screened nearby catch basins, located on the Bickfords 

property (526 North Main Street) and in the North Main Street Right of Way.  The utility 

manhole screening process utilizes a hand held PID meter to evaluate for the presence of 

airborne photoionizable volatile organic compounds (VOCs).  This screening process produces 

“real time” readings total VOCs in the air.  The PID meter does not identify or quantify specific 

VOCs but provides a readout concentration of all photoionizable compounds within the range of 

the ultraviolet lamp of the meter. The PID meter utilizes a 10.6 electron Volt (eV) lamp 

calibrated to a 100 ppmv isobutylene standard.  Field screening results were 0.0 ppmv in all 

catch basins.  No oily liquids or gasoline odors were observed during the air screening of the 

utility manholes. 

 

Based on current information, PMPs have not been identified at the Site.  The on-Site building is 

currently vacant.  As discussed in Section 5.2 on-Site soil gas points were screened during the 

March 2014 groundwater sampling event.  In the event that it is occupied, soil vapor points may 

be installed within the footprint of the building to assess the vapor intrusion pathway.  The Site 

will continue to be assessed and monitored for PMPs.
